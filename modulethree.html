LASER CUTTING

What is Laser Cutting?

Laser cutting is a technology that uses a laser to vaporize materials, resulting in a cut edge. While typically used for industrial manufacturing applications, it is now used by schools, small businesses, architecture, and hobbyists. Laser cutting works by directing the output of a high-power laser most commonly through optics. The laser optics and CNC (computer numerical control) are used to direct the laser beam to the material. A commercial laser for cutting materials uses a motion control system to follow a CNC or G-code of the pattern to be cut onto the material.
Laser Cutting is a very meticulous process. It requires Laser Cutting Machine as well as a software to guide the machine. The one used in our lab is developed by Suresh Indu Lasers Pvt. Ltd. There are many software options available such as LightBurn, LaserCAD, CorelDRAW, Adobe Illustrator, etc. The software we are using in the lab is LASERCAD.

Why LaserCAD?

LaserCAD is a leading software solution for laser cutting and engraving, often provided by manufacturers of laser cutting machines. It offers vector drawing tools for creating intricate designs, supports image processing for detailed engraving, and includes a material library for optimal cutting parameters. With a user-friendly interface and compatibility with specific laser cutting machines, LaserCAD streamlines the design and production process, making it a comprehensive tool for laser cutting applications.

Guided by our instructors, we successfully created our first ever laser cut. 2D laser cutting is a highly precise and efficient manufacturing process used across various industries for cutting flat sheet materials such as metal, plastic, wood, and composites. It involves the use of a high-powered laser beam to vaporize, melt, or burn away material along a predetermined path, guided by a computer-controlled system. The laser beam is focused through a lens to achieve a small spot size, resulting in intricate and accurate cuts with minimal heat-affected zones. This process offers several advantages, including high speed, repeatability, and the ability to cut complex shapes without the need for tool changes. Additionally, it can be easily automated, making it suitable for both prototyping and large-scale production.

It's great for making custom signs, decorations, or adding logos to products because it's precise and can create really intricate designs. It's kind of like printing, but instead of ink, you're using a laser to make cool pictures on stuff!

SUMMARY OF THIS MODULE


Laser cutting is a highly precise and versatile manufacturing process that utilizes a focused laser beam to cut through various materials with exceptional accuracy. The process involves a computer-controlled system guiding the laser beam along the desired cutting path, which can range from simple geometric shapes to intricate designs. One of the key advantages of laser cutting is its ability to cut through a wide range of materials, including metals, plastics, wood, glass, and composites, with minimal heat-affected zones. This allows for clean and precise cuts without the need for additional finishing processes. Laser cutting is widely used across industries such as automotive, aerospace, electronics, and jewelry for applications such as part fabrication, prototyping, signage, and artistic creations. The versatility of laser cutting extends beyond simple cutting operations. It can also be used for engraving, marking, and etching designs onto the surface of materials. This makes laser cutting an ideal choice for adding logos, serial numbers, or decorative patterns to products and components. Additionally, laser cutting offers advantages in terms of speed and efficiency compared to traditional cutting methods. With advancements in laser technology, including the development of high-power fiber lasers and CO2 lasers, manufacturers can achieve even faster cutting speeds and higher precision. Moreover, laser cutting is a highly automated process, which allows for increased productivity and reduced labor costs. Computer numerical control (CNC) technology ensures repeatability and consistency in cutting operations, making it suitable for both small-scale production and large-scale manufacturing. Furthermore, laser cutting is environmentally friendly, as it produces minimal waste and does not require the use of harmful chemicals or solvents. In conclusion, laser cutting is a sophisticated manufacturing process that offers exceptional precision, versatility, and efficiency. Its ability to cut through various materials and create intricate designs makes it indispensable across a wide range of industries. With ongoing advancements in laser technology, laser cutting continues to evolve, providing manufacturers with innovative solutions for their cutting and engraving needs.
